# Simple RubriQ User Guide

Welcome to Simple RubriQ – your intelligent GCSE marking assistant. This guide will help you get started and make the most of the platform.

## Getting Started

### Creating Your Account
1. Visit the Simple RubriQ website
2. Click **Sign Up** and enter your email address
3. Create a secure password
4. Verify your email address
5. Log in to access your dashboard

### Your First Login
After logging in, you'll see your Dashboard – your central hub for all marking activities.

## Setting Up Your First Rubric

### Using GCSE Templates (Recommended)
1. Click **Rubrics** in the navigation menu
2. Choose your **Exam Board** (AQA, Edexcel, OCR, or WJEC)
3. Select a **GCSE Template** that matches your subject
4. The rubric criteria will automatically populate
5. Review and adjust if needed
6. Click **Save Rubric**

### Importing Your Own Rubric
1. Click **Rubrics** in the navigation menu
2. Click **Choose File** in the upload section
3. Upload a `.txt` or `.docx` file containing your rubric
4. Review the imported criteria
5. Make any adjustments
6. Click **Save Rubric**

### Creating a Custom Rubric
1. Click **Rubrics** in the navigation menu
2. Select your **Subject** from the dropdown
3. Enter a **Rubric Name**
4. Add **Criteria** by clicking "Add Criterion"
5. For each criterion:
   - Enter the category name (e.g., "Content", "Grammar")
   - Set the maximum points
6. Click **Save Rubric**

## Marking Essays

### Single Essay Marking

#### Step 1: Open Essay Feedback
- Click **Essay Feedback** in the navigation menu

#### Step 2: Enter Essay Content
- Paste the student's essay into the text area
- Or type/load it directly

#### Step 3: Generate AI Feedback
- Click the **Generate Feedback** button (or press `Ctrl+Enter`)
- Wait a few seconds for AI analysis
- Review the generated feedback in three sections:
  - **Strengths**: What the student did well
  - **Areas for Improvement**: Specific suggestions
  - **Grammar Issues**: Writing mechanics

#### Step 4: Customize Feedback
Use the **Comment Bank** to add quick, professional comments:
1. Click **Comment Bank** to expand
2. Filter by:
   - **AO** (Assessment Objective): AO1, AO2, AO3, or AO4
   - **Category**: Strength, Improvement, or SPAG
3. Search for specific phrases
4. Click **Insert** to add to feedback
5. Or click **Copy** to paste elsewhere

#### Step 5: Review AO Breakdown
- Toggle **Show AO Legend** to see Assessment Objective definitions
- Review the band analysis showing AO1–AO4 scores
- Adjust the overall score using arrow buttons or direct entry

#### Step 6: Export Feedback
- Click **Export to PDF** (or press `Ctrl+Shift+P`) for a formatted report
- Click **Export to DOCX** (or press `Ctrl+Shift+D`) for an editable document
- The AO Legend will be included if toggled on

### Batch Processing

#### When to Use Batch Mode
- Multiple essays from the same assignment
- End-of-term assessments
- Mock exam scripts

#### How to Use Batch Upload
1. Click **Batch** in the navigation menu
2. Prepare your essays (CSV format or multiple files)
3. Toggle **AI pre-mark on upload**:
   - **On**: Automatic AI feedback for all essays (faster, uses AI credits)
   - **Off**: Upload only, mark manually later (cost-saving option)
4. Click **Upload** and select your files
5. Monitor the progress indicator
6. Review and refine individual feedback as needed

## Calibration & Quality Control

### What is Calibration?
Calibration helps you ensure consistent marking across different essays or between multiple markers. It's especially useful for moderation and quality assurance.

### Creating a Calibration Session
1. Click **Calibration** in the navigation menu
2. Enter a **Session Name** (e.g., "November Mock Scripts")
3. Select the **Rubric** you're using
4. Choose up to **10 sample essays**
5. Click **Create Session**

### Marking in a Calibration Session
1. Click on an essay to open it
2. Read the content
3. Enter your AO scores (AO1, AO2, AO3, AO4)
4. Click **Submit Mark**
5. Repeat for other essays

### Understanding Agreement Stats
After multiple markers score the same essays, you'll see:
- **Average Score** per AO: The mean score across all markers
- **Standard Deviation (SD)**: How much markers agree
  - **Low SD** (0-2): Strong consistency ✓
  - **High SD** (3+): Criteria may need clarification

Use these insights to refine your rubrics and marking guidelines.

## Rubric Management

### Cloning a Rubric
When you want to revise a rubric without losing the original:
1. Go to **Rubrics**
2. Find the rubric you want to clone
3. Click **Clone**
4. A new version (v2, v3, etc.) is created
5. Edit the new version as needed

### Viewing Rubric History
1. Click **Lineage** next to any rubric
2. See all versions in chronological order
3. Compare criteria counts and exam boards
4. Track when changes were made

This helps with:
- Audit trails for moderation
- Understanding how criteria evolved
- Reverting to previous versions if needed

## Understanding Assessment Objectives (AO)

### What are AOs?
Assessment Objectives are the official marking criteria used in GCSE English:

- **AO1**: Content & Ideas
- **AO2**: Structure & Organization
- **AO3**: Language & Vocabulary
- **AO4**: Grammar & Accuracy

### How Simple RubriQ Uses AOs
- AI feedback is organized by AO
- Band analysis shows performance per AO
- Comment bank is filtered by AO
- Calibration tracks consistency per AO
- PDF exports include AO explanations (when toggled on)

## Tips for Best Results

### For Accurate AI Feedback
- Ensure essays are at least 150 words
- Include the full essay text (not excerpts)
- Select the correct rubric before generating feedback
- Review AI suggestions – they're a starting point, not final word

### For Efficient Marking
1. Use templates for recurring assignments
2. Build your comment bank with custom snippets
3. Enable keyboard shortcuts
4. Use batch mode for multiple essays
5. Clone rubrics instead of recreating from scratch

### For Consistent Quality
- Run calibration sessions quarterly
- Refine rubrics when SD is high
- Use the lineage feature to track improvements
- Export feedback as PDF for official records

## Getting Help

### Common Issues

**Q: AI feedback seems generic**
- Make sure your rubric is detailed and specific
- Try adding more context in the essay prompt
- Use the comment bank to add personalized touches

**Q: Can't export PDF**
- Ensure feedback has been generated first
- Check your browser allows downloads
- Try a different browser if issues persist

**Q: Batch upload fails**
- Verify file format (CSV or supported text files)
- Check file size limits
- Ensure proper column headers in CSV

**Q: Keyboard shortcuts not working**
- Make sure you're focused in the essay feedback area
- Check for browser extensions blocking shortcuts
- Press `Shift + ?` to see the help panel
